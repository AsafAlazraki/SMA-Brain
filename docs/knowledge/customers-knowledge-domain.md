## 2. The knowledge curriculum an industry expert holds

1. **Machine classes & feed types:** drop feed, needle feed, walking foot, compound/unison (triple) feed, puller feed, differential feed (overlockers), top-and-bottom feed. Body formats: flatbed, cylinder arm, post bed, long arm, feed-off-the-arm. Specialty classes: zigzag, bartack, buttonhole, button-sew, blindstitch, tape-edge, pattern sewers. Canonical explainers: [Sailrite feeding mechanisms](https://www.sailrite.com/Comparing-Sewing-Machine-Feeding-Mechanisms), [Textile Learner](https://textilelearner.net/sewing-machine-feed-mechanism/).
2. **Stitch types (ISO 4915 / ASTM D6193):** 101 single-thread chain, 301 lockstitch, 304 zigzag, 401 two-thread chain, 406/407 bottom coverstitch, 503/504/505 overedge, 512/514 mock safety, 516 safety stitch, 602/605 top-and-bottom coverstitch. Definitive one-pager: [A&E Stitch Type Matrix PDF](https://www.amefird.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/Stitch-Type-Matrix.pdf).
3. **Needle systems & sizes:** DBx1 (industrial lockstitch), DPx5, DPx17/135x17 (walking foot), DCx27/B27 (overlock), UY128GAS (coverstitch), 134/135x5, 190, 794/7x3 (441-class), 29-BL (blindstitch); Nm vs Singer sizing; point styles (round vs cutting points LR/S/D for leather). Refs: [Thread Exchange](https://www.thethreadexchange.com/miva/merchant.mvc?Screen=CTGY&Category_Code=NDL-SYS), [Groz-Beckert catalogue](https://portal.groz-beckert.com/8600/sewing/en/products).
4. **Thread numbering & selection:** Tex, ticket (Tkt), denier, V-size, Government sizes + conversions ([Coats](https://www.coats.com/en-us/info-hub/thread-numbering/), [ServiceThread](https://www.servicethread.com/blog/what-is-the-difference-between-ticket-size-and-tex-size-for-bonded-thread)); bonded nylon vs poly vs PTFE ([Techsew](https://www.techsew.com/us/blog/nylon-thread-buying-guide.html)); thread→needle pairing with break strengths V33→V554 ([Toledo](https://www.tolindsewmach.com/thread-chart.html)).
5. **Machine setup & adjustment:** needle-bar height, needle-to-hook timing, feed-dog height/timing, tension balance, presser pressure, safety clutch reset, zigzag cam timing — codified per model in OEM **engineer's manuals** (distinct from operator manuals): [Juki DDL-8700-7 Engineer's Manual](https://www.manualslib.com/manual/1087074/Juki-Ddl-8700-7.html), [LU-1500N PDF](https://semsi.com.mx/Manuales/JUKI/LU-1500N%20%20SEM02_.pdf).
6. **Faults & troubleshooting (the canonical fault set):** skipped stitches (wrong needle system/size, deflection, timing, thread), thread breakage, birdnesting, puckering, uneven stitch length, not feeding, noise/jams. Refs: [ServiceThread skipped stitches](https://www.servicethread.com/blog/industrial-sewing-how-to-avoid-skipped-stitches), [mechanic's decision-tree flowchart](https://sewingmachineman.substack.com/p/skipped-stitches-troubleshooting), [A&E maintenance checklist PDF](https://www.amefird.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/Sewing-Mantenance-Check-List-2-5-10.pdf), [garment defect cause/remedy lists](https://sewingmachinegreeks.com/list-of-garment-defects-causes-and-remedies-with-images/).
7. **Servicing & intervals:** daily de-lint/oil check; heuristics: daily-use machines ~6-monthly service, weekly-use annually; annual for industrials ([Airtasker AU](https://www.airtasker.com/handyman/sewing-machine-repair/price/sewing-machine-repair-cost/), [QLD Sewing](https://qldsewingmachines.com.au/industrial-services-2/), [Konsew PM checklist](https://konsew.co.uk/blog/preventative-maintenance-checklist-for-industrial-sewing-machines)).
8. **Parts, attachments & accessories:** hooks (horizontal/vertical axis, M-bobbin), bobbin cases, feet (welting/piping, zipper, Teflon), feed dogs/plates, knives/loopers, folders/binders/hemmers (right-angle binders, lap-seam fellers, double-fold hemmers) ([Universal Sewing](https://www.universalsewing.com/hemmers-binders-and-seam-folders), [Atlanta Attachment](https://www.atlatt.com/product-category/folders/)).
9. **Motors, tables, lubrication:** clutch vs servo (servo: quiet, controllable, energy-saving, needle-positioner options) ([Goldstar](https://www.goldstartool.com/blog/pros-and-cons-of-sewing-machine-motors.htm), [Sailrite upgrade guide](https://www.sailrite.com/How-to-Upgrade-Your-Clutch-Motor-to-a-Workhorse-Servo-Motor)); K-leg tables; auto-lube sump vs manual-oil.

**Where this knowledge lives today:** OEM engineer's manuals & parts books (ManualsLib, [Superior Sewing catalogs/diagrams](https://www.supsew.com/catalogs-diagrams/), [College Sewing parts books](https://www.college-sewing.co.uk/parts-books/global-parts-books.html), [free parts lists](https://www.industrialsewingmachineparts.co.uk/partslists.htm), [Henderson](https://www.hendersonsewing.com/manual.asp)); forums (leatherworker.net = deepest corpus, PatternReview, Quiltingboard, Sailing Anarchy, The Upholstery Forum, r/myog, r/sewing); Facebook mechanic groups ([Industrial Sewing Machine Mechanics](https://www.facebook.com/groups/industrialsewingmachinemechanics/)); YouTube ([Sailrite](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCLEyuinra_2j8-SKvDX33Vw) — the model of a dealer winning through free how-to content, [Alexander Dyer](https://www.youtube.com/@AlexanderDyer)); trainer providers ([Sewing Machines Institute](https://www.sewingmachinesinstitute.com/), [Sewing Doc Academy](https://www.sewingdocacademy.com/), [Artizan UK](https://artizantraining.com/) — no formal AU qualification); supplier education hubs (A&E, Coats, Superior); vintage archives (ISMACS, Internet Archive).

## 3. Troubleshooting & service economics (Australia)

**Pricing benchmarks:**
- Airtasker AU: repairs $15–$200, median ~$90; computerised ~$160, long-arm ~$140, vintage $200+ ([guide](https://www.airtasker.com/handyman/sewing-machine-repair/price/sewing-machine-repair-cost/)).
- Mike's Sewing Machine Repairs: domestic services $90–$165; overlockers $140–$165; coverstitch $140 ([menu](https://www.mikessewingmachinerepairs.com.au/sewing-machine-service-price-menu/)).
- QLD Sewing Machines (Loganholme): assessment $49–$59; services $159–$219; overlockers $169–$199; quick-fix from $29; turnaround 7–10 business days; industrial = hourly labour + travel, parts extra; **24/7 phone tech support advertised** ([industrial](https://qldsewingmachines.com.au/industrial-services-2/)).
- Mobile/onsite techs thin on the ground: Joe Galang (Brisbane, "24 hour"), Sunshine Coast Mobile Sewing Machine Mechanic, SewMasters Brisbane.

**Skills shortage / aging technicians:**
- "Demise of the Australian Sewing Machine Mechanic" ([John Grom essay](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/demise-australian-sewing-machine-mechanic-john-grom)); "mechanics seem to be disappearing" ([PatternReview](https://sewing.patternreview.com/SewingDiscussions/topic/128658)); pandemic-era repair-professional shortage coverage ([Seven Days](https://m.sevendaysvt.com/news/the-pandemic-highlights-a-shortage-of-sewing-machine-repair-professionals-34646958)); Artizan: "industry is desperate for skilled mechanics" (~100M machines worldwide needing repair); Juki America: "shortage of qualified technicians and teachers… has been a huge burden" ([Texintel](https://www.texintel.com/press-room/juki-brings-a.i.-power-to-the-reshoring-fashion-sewing-factory)); SEEK maintains a live AU "sewing machine mechanic" jobs category.

**The dealer support burden:**
- SMA runs a structured pre-sales intake ("New Industrial Sewing Machine Enquiry") to triage the which-machine question ([SMA](https://www.sewingmachinesaustralia.com.au/new-industrial-sewing-machine)).
- Dealers publish the same explainer canon because the same questions recur ([Jacksew "which machine"](https://www.jacksew.com/blog/which-sewing-machine-is-right-for-your-business/), [Techsew leather picker](https://www.techsew.com/blogs/techsew-blog/how-to-pick-the-right-leather-sewing-machine), [Sailrite feed comparison](https://www.sailrite.com/Comparing-Sewing-Machine-Feeding-Mechanisms)).
- QLD Sewing's 24/7 phone support = direct evidence after-hours troubleshooting calls are real workload.
- Direct-import buyers still consume dealer-grade support: consensus on leatherworker.net is the AliExpress buyer "become[s] the 'dealer' for servicing purposes… responsible for all repairs, adjustments and parts costs" ([thread](https://leatherworker.net/forum/topic/82636-sewing-machine-from-china/)).

## 4. Trends

1. **Onshore textile manufacturing revival:** National Manufacturing Strategy for Australian Fashion & Textiles 2026–2036 launched 16 Mar 2026 (AFC + R.M.Williams, Parliament House): value-add $2.6B → $2.9B target by 2030-31; 27,000+ TCF workers (58% women); ~97% made offshore today; +1,000 skilled jobs, +$864M wages projected over 5 yrs ([Australian Manufacturing](https://www.australianmanufacturing.com.au/national-strategy-targets-growth-in-australias-fashion-and-textile-manufacturing/), [Inside Retail](https://insideretail.com.au/business/supply-chain/fashions-plan-to-bring-manufacturing-home-to-australia-202605)).
2. **Caravan/RV & marine demand (canvas-adjacent):** 23,963 RVs manufactured 2025 (12% above 2019), caravans 18,438 (+7.3%); fleet 937K; $12.6B domestic spend ([Caravan Industry Assoc SOTI 2026](https://www.caravanindustry.com.au/state-of-the-industry-2026-resilience-in-a-changing-market/)). Marine: $10.2B+ turnover, ~35K employees ([BIA](https://bia.org.au/library/industry-data/)). Every van/boat = seats, annexes, covers, clears, canvas — walking-foot territory.
3. **Small-batch makers / Etsy economy:** ~5.6–8M+ active Etsy sellers globally; AU retailers merchandise industrial overlock/coverstitch to label founders ([Know-How](https://knowhowsewing.com.au/collections/6-industrial-sewing-machines-amp-overlockers)).
4. **Quilting/long-arm:** ≈US$4.5B annualized 2025; 9–11M US quilters; Handi Quilter has dedicated AU arm + dealer network ([Craft Industry Alliance](https://craftindustryalliance.org/the-size-of-the-quilting-market-quilting-trends-survey-results-2025/)).
5. **Automation & programmable machines:** Juki AMS-210/221/224/251 pattern sewers; JaNets production monitoring; **ShuHaRi free maintenance app** (step-by-step daily maintenance + paid operator e-learning) — Juki's response to reshoring + technician shortage ([Texintel](https://www.texintel.com/press-room/juki-brings-a.i.-power-to-the-reshoring-fashion-sewing-factory)); mattress most automated ([Atlanta Attachment](https://www.atlatt.com/product-category/mattress/)).
6. **Direct-from-China buying problems:** buyer becomes their own dealer; freight/taxes/240V conversion erase savings; drop-shipper model substitution; clone quality variance; "cheap tools are expensive" ([leatherworker.net](https://leatherworker.net/forum/topic/82636-sewing-machine-from-china/)) — core differentiation narrative for a dealer knowledge-bot.
7. **Right to repair:** Productivity Commission inquiry (Report 97, Oct 2021) recommended improving access to repair supplies, parts, information ([pc.gov.au](https://www.pc.gov.au/inquiries-and-research/repair/)) — tailwind for dealers who publish knowledge rather than gatekeep.

## 5. Existing AI/chatbot/knowledge tools — and the gap

**Sewing-specific AI that exists (all consumer-craft oriented):**
- **Sew Smart Assistants** (Sew Smart AI, LLC): six subscription AI "experts" incl. "Mac" for machines/troubleshooting — US$2.99/mo or $29.99/yr, browser-based ([sewsmartai.com](https://sewsmartai.com/)). Hobbyist-focused; no industrial depth, no parts lookup, no dealer tie-in.
- **SewingGPT** — generic GPT wrapper ([YesChat](https://www.yeschat.ai/gpts-2OTocFyJRD-SewingGPT)).
- **Manualzz "AI Chat & PDF"** over hosted manuals incl. Juki engineer's manuals — generic single-doc RAG ([Manualzz](https://manualzz.com/doc/62005241/juki-ddl-9000a-engineer-s-manual)).
- **OEM software:** Juki ShuHaRi + JaNets; SVP Worldwide's 2022 consumer "first AI sewing machine with voice control" ([Etteplan](https://www.etteplan.com/about-us/news/2022/06/29/the-worlds-first-sewing-machine-with-artificial-intelligence-and-voice-control-features-developed-by-svp-worldwide-and-etteplan/)).
- AI-generated troubleshooting content farms (low-trust, generic).

**Non-AI knowledge/parts infrastructure (raw material for the Brain):** Superior Sewing catalogs & diagrams + digital library, College Sewing parts books, industrialsewingmachineparts.co.uk free parts lists, Henderson manuals, Jacksew parts store; manual repositories (ISMACS, ManualsLib, freesewingmachinemanuals.com, Internet Archive); reference charts (Toledo, A&E stitch matrix, Thread Exchange, Coats); dealer content moats (Sailrite Learn + YouTube = category benchmark); adjacent-industry precedent: CNH's AI tool for dealer/customer support in agriculture ([Farm Equipment](https://www.farm-equipment.com/articles/23385-cnh-launches-artificial-intelligence-ai-tool-for-dealer-and-customer-support)); ChatMRO AI parts finding across 30M+ industrial SKUs ([chatmro.com](https://chatmro.com/)).

**THE GAP (nothing like this exists anywhere):** no industrial-sewing-specific, dealer-operated AI assistant combining (a) segment-aware machine recommendation, (b) model-specific troubleshooting grounded in engineer's manuals, (c) parts-book lookup by model, (d) thread/needle/consumable matching, (e) Australian service logistics (pricing, turnaround, onsite norms, phone-triage deflection). Every ingredient exists scattered across PDFs, forums, YouTube, US/UK supplier sites; nobody has assembled it. The aging-technician problem plus right-to-repair tailwind makes captured, queryable expert knowledge the scarcest asset in the trade.
