## 4. Market size & structure

**Machine market (IndexBox modelled estimates — treat with caution; conflicting CAGRs +0.2% vs +4.1% across posts):**
- Industrial, 2024: consumption ~106K units / US$91M wholesale, down 4.1% (vol) YoY; peak 2014 (129K / $121M); imports 4.2K units / US$4.9M with **China 58% share**; avg import price ~US$1,200/unit ([IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/industrial-sewing-machine-australia-market-overview-2024-2/)).
- Household, 2024: consumption ~218K units / US$22M; **imports 241K units — China 123K, Vietnam 67K, Thailand 23K = 88% of volume**; import prices US$31/unit (China) to ~US$394 (Japan); re-exports ~28K to PNG/NZ/Fiji ([IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/household-sewing-machine-australia-market-overview-2024-4/)). HS code 8452 ([OEC](https://oec.world/en/profile/hs/sewing-machines)).
- **No standalone IBISWorld AU sewing-machine industry report**; nearest = company profiles + adjacent industries (Clothing Retailing, Footwear Mfg, Wooden Furniture & Upholstered Seat Mfg).

**Buyer base (TCF & sewn-products mfg):**
- AFC/Epson/RMIT 2024: Australian fashion manufacturing **lost 18% of its value in five years**; Victorian TCF worth >$930M (2023), 9,000+ workers; unchecked, −700 jobs/−$98M by 2030 ([texfash](https://texfash.com/update/new-report-warns-of-18-decline-in-value-of-australian-fashion-industry)).
- **Sewing machinists: only ~6,100 employed nationally, shrinking ~400/yr, median age 53, 84% female**, NSW 30%/VIC 22%/QLD 21% ([Jobs & Skills Australia](https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/data/occupation-and-industry-profiles/occupations/7116-sewing-machinists)).
- Demand migrated from garment factories to **upholstery, marine trimming/canvas, shade sails, leather goods, horse rugs, sailmaking, curtains/blinds, bag/packaging closing**. Marine/canvas fabricators organised under Specialised Textiles Association Marine Division ([specialisedtextiles.com.au](https://www.specialisedtextiles.com.au/about-us/marine-fabricators-division/)). Reshoring push: National Manufacturing Strategy for Fashion & Textiles 2026–2036 (AFC + R.M.Williams, launched Mar 2026): value-add $2.6B → target $2.9B by 2030-31 ([Australian Manufacturing](https://www.australianmanufacturing.com.au/national-strategy-targets-growth-in-australias-fashion-and-textile-manufacturing/), [Ragtrader](https://www.ragtrader.com.au/news/afc-r-m-williams-unveil-plan-to-revive-local-fashion-production)).

## 5. Industry economics

**Price points (new, AUD, incl. table/motor where stated):**
- Jack H2-A-CZ walking foot **$1,900**; Jack A5E-A-7 auto lockstitch **$2,450**; Jack A6F-E-Q **$2,800**; Jack C7 4-thread overlock **$2,800**; heavy compound H6-CZXB-4 **$2,850** ([S&P price list](https://sandpsewingsupplies.com.au/collections/jack-industrial-machines)).
- Jack H2-CZ at SMW: **$2,695 inc GST**, 74 kg head ([SMW](https://www.sewingmachinewarehouse.com.au/new-industrial-sewing-machines/jack-h2cz-industrial-walking-foot-sewing-machine/)).
- Japanese benchmark: Juki DDL-8700 **~$2,500** w/ bench + motor ([Blackmore & Roy](https://blackmoreandroy.com.au/product/juki-ddl-8700-single-needle-straight-sewing-lockstitch-machine/)).
- Specialty/heavy leather (Cowboy CB3200/6900 class) and premium German automated (DA M-/H-Type) sit well above — quote-based. No published AU margin data anywhere in the trade.
- Avg industrial import unit value ~US$1.2K vs retail ~A$2–3K indicates the landed-to-retail spread before table/motor assembly, freight, warranty.

**Technician shortage — hard evidence:**
- **~250 Textile, Clothing & Footwear Mechanics nationally (ANZSCO 323215), median age 57, 38.7% aged 55+**, VIC 40%/QLD 26% ([Jobs & Skills Australia](https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/data/occupation-and-industry-profiles/occupations/323215-textile-clothing-and-footwear-mechanics)).
- Skilled-migration eligible (189/190/491) ([anzscosearch](https://www.anzscosearch.com/323215/)).
- **No dedicated apprenticeship** — QLD routes via engineering Cert III/IV (MEM30219 etc.); TAFE offers operator-level skill sets only. Dealers grow their own (Echidna advertising an Apprentice Sewing Machine Technician; SMA had a trainee technician).
- "Demise of the Australian Sewing Machine Mechanic" (John Grom, LinkedIn essay) ([link](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/demise-australian-sewing-machine-mechanic-john-grom)).

**Parts supply chain:** genuine Japanese/German parts via brand agents; generic/aftermarket + Groz-Beckert/Schmetz needles via specialist stockists — Marfar the visible national parts house; dealers (Elizabeth, SMA) run their own parts stores. Machine heads predominantly China-made (incl. Juki China/Vietnam plants; Typical claims to OEM for Brother, Mitsubishi, Pfaff, Consew, Seiko per Camsew).

**Freight:** heads 70–80 kg + flat-packed tables; dealers sell Australia-wide with freight networks; vigorous second-hand market via dealer used stock, Gumtree, Facebook Marketplace.

## 7. Key structural takeaways for SMA

1. **Exclusive-agency moats:** Juki industrial (Elizabeth), DA (DASEC), Typical (Camsew), Highlead (Highlead Aus), Handi Quilter (Blessington) are locked. SMA competes largely in the open-import Chinese-brand space (Siruba, Zoje, house-brand reworks) plus DA/Juki retail and its service reputation.
2. **Garment-factory customer base structurally shrinking** (18% value loss in 5 years; machinists −400/yr) — dealers survive on upholstery/marine/leather/equine/packaging niches, servicing, parts, and prosumer overlap.
3. **Servicing is the scarcest asset** — ~250 qualified mechanics nationally, median age 57, no apprenticeship pipeline. SMA's founder-technician knowledge is both its key defensible capability and its succession risk — exactly what the Brain project preserves.
4. Real competitor set: Elizabeth Machines, DASEC, QLD Sewing Machines, Sewing Machine City, Camsew, MJC, Peter Industrial, Highlead Aus, SMW, Know-How, Kameo, Marfar (parts), leather specialists (Sun Valley/Cowboy, Cobra via QLD Sewing).
